When the World Series of Poker announced they would try to have a main event online and play the final table in a live setting, everyone knew the risk. The WSOP told players that if anyone at the final table tested positive for Covid-19 they would automatically be disqualified from the tournament. Many people in the poker world thought this was irresponsible of the World Series to even try to run a live televised final table but here we are.

Upeshka De Silva (the player with the most poker accolades at the final table), has been disqualified from the World Series of Poker Main Event and will automatically get 9th place in the tournament.

De Silva says that he quarantined on December 10th to prevent something like this from happening. He tested positive on Dec. 20th, negative on Dec. 26th, and positive on Dec. 27th.

De Silva will be awarded 9th place and take home $98,813.

Some popular poker players took to Twitter after the news was released:

One of the final 9 for the @WSOP main event tomorrow at the Rio has tested positive for Covid-19 and has been disqualified.

Upeshka De Silva aka @PadawanPesh says he quarantined on Dec 10th - tested positive on the 20th, negative on 26th, and positive on the 27th

— Joey Ingram #PASSION (@Joeingram1) December 28, 2020

This is finally the time for players to come together and let their voices be heard. If wsop final tablists sit out until Pesh is back/a fair solution is made it would be a industry changing turn.

— Patrick Leonard (@padspoker) December 28, 2020

The WSOP is unlikely to change their decision. This is a nationally ESPN televised event that has too much on the line for the World Series of Poker brand. The only way the ruling won't stand is if the other 8 players at the final table decide not to play because of it (this is also unlikely to happen).
